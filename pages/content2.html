Capturing & Controlling Thoughts
The Merriam-Webster dictionary defines self-control as “restraint exercised over one's own impulses, emotions, or desires.” We feel all sorts of emotions every day, and it's important to express them. But learning how to let go of control is also important.
How to control your thoughts?
  • Practice mindfulness meditation and breathing exercises.
  • Take a pause during your day to slow your mind down.
  • Make sure you rest your mind properly after exercising or on a stressful day.
  • DStop listening to negative, pessimistic people and the advice they give you.
  • Include positive affirmations in your self-talk rather than put-downs.
Why is it important to control your thoughts?
Having control over our thoughts helps us achieve our short-term and long-term goals. It helps us get through our days professionally and personally with a clear, confident, growth mindset. It's hard to achieve our goals when our minds are under attack — especially when the attack comes from within ourselves.
Personal Resilience
Personal resilience is about self-awareness, coping strategies, getting the right support and being positive. Self-awareness, strategies and support help you to cope. Feeling able to cope builds your confidence and helps you to develop a positive outlook.
3 Steps to Improve your Personal Resilience
  • Identify your Personal,Family and Social Support.
  • Dump the Drains - Protect yourself.
  • Map your Experience - Looking back to learn.
How can people build resilience in their personal life?
Develop a good sleep routine , try out a new exercise or use physical relaxation techniques,like deep breathing or meditation. Practice thought awareness.Resilient people don't let negative thoughts derail their efforts. Instead, they consistently practice positive thinking.
Self Talk
Self-talk is the way you talk to yourself, or your inner voice. You might not be aware that you're doing it, but you almost certainly are. This inner voice combines conscious thoughts with inbuilt beliefs and biases to create an internal monologue throughout the day.
Tips to stop negative self-talk
  • Challenge your thoughts. Ask yourself — is it true?
  • Put your thoughts into perspective (so what?).
  • Replace the thought with a neutral or positive thought.
What are the benefits of positive self-talk?
  • Improve self-esteem, stress management and wellbeing.
  • Reduce any symptoms of depression, anxiety and personality disorders.
  • Make you feel more in control of your life.
